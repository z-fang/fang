Sharing economy: welfare and revenue

In the last decade, the sharing economy has upended a wide variety of industries, from bike sharing and car sharing to food delivery and hotels. However, despite the rapid rise and success of online sharing platforms, there is much about their design and operation that is yet to be understood.
We study the optimal pricing strategy for a sharing platform, under the objectives of maximizing revenue and social welfare, given strategic users with general utility functions. We show that welfare maximization and revenue maximization are aligned when supply is insufficient. We also show how platform subsidies help increase sharing supply.

 

Recent publications:
Zhixuan Fang, Longbo Huang and Adam Wierman. “Loyalty Programs in the Sharing Economy: Optimality and Competition.” Proceedings of the 19th ACM International Symposium on Mobile Ad Hoc Networking and Computing (MobiHoc), 2018.

Market equilibrium: when brand name matters

We investigate the brand name effect in a competitive market. We propose a variant Hotelling model and take into account the brand name effect on customers behavior.
We show that a firm's pricing power at market equilibrium is propotional to its market share over its competition intensity. We also show how the brand name effect intensifies the competitions for small companies.

 

Recent publications:
Zhixuan Fang, and Longbo Huang. “Market Share Analysis with Brand Effect.” Proceedings of IEEE Conference on Decision and Control (CDC), 2016.
